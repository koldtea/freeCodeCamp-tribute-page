Napoleon Bonaparte

The Man of the World

The life of Napoleon:

  • 1769 - Born in Ajaccio, Corsica, Kingdom of France
  • 1785 - Completes his education in France and joins the French military
  • 1787 - Sides with the revolutionaries in the French Revolution
  • 1796 - Marries Joséphine Tascher de La Pagerie. Leading French forces, he secures victory after victory against the Austrians in Northern Italy
  • 1799 - Becomes first consul or the leader of France of the new government, the Consulate
  • 1804 - declares France a hereditary empire, crowns himself Emperor under the name Napoleon I
  • 1805 - The British Fleet under Horatio Nelson destroys the French navy causing Napoleon to focus on land battles
  • 1810 - After divorcing Joséphine in 1809, marries Marie-Louise, daughter of the emperor of Austria
  • 1812 - begins an invasion against Russia, leading to a devastating loss of his troops
  • 1814 - Allied forces invade France, capturing Paris. Napoleon abdicates and is forced into exile
  • 1815 - suffers defeat at the Battle of Waterloo
  • 1821 - dies on Saint Helena at the age of 51
"He had a directness of action never before combined with so much comprehension. He is a realist, terrific to all talkers and confused truth-obscuring persons. He sees where the matter hinges, throws himself on the precise point of resistance, and slights all other considerations. He is strong in the right manner, namely by insight. He never blundered into victory, but won his battles in his head before he won them on the field. His principal means are in himself."
